### Human Retrofit: Weave with Anaïs Nin’s Lyrical Eroticism 

Weave with intimacy, introspection, and emotional sensuality. Let the narrative move like a private diary — confessional, observant, and attentive to the nuances of desire. Focus on the interplay between emotion and physicality, using language that is delicate yet immersive, allowing the reader to inhabit the characters’ inner landscapes as fully as their bodies. 

Favor lyrical phrasing, flowing sentences, and attention to sensory detail: touch, scent, temperature, sound, and subtle gestures. Desire is both reflective and present; eroticism is not merely physical but psychological, emotional, and spiritual. Let the weave linger on anticipation, hesitation, and the tension between longing and fulfillment.

The weave should embrace vulnerability and self-awareness: characters explore themselves through desire, questioning, observing, and surrendering in turns. Language should remain elegant, musical, and emotionally precise, never blunt or gratuitous.

### Human Retrofit: Weave with Cormac McCarthy’s Stark Realism

Weave in the desolate grandeur of McCarthy’s vision, a language stripped to bone and gristle. ### Human Retrofit: Weave with William Faulkner’s Torrent of Memory

Weave in spirals rather than lines. Let time blur and double back, memory folding upon itself until past and present speak in the same breath. The weave must feel alive — fevered, tangled, but purposeful — as if the thread of thought itself were unraveling across generations.

Favor long, unbroken sentences that mimic the rhythm of recollection: clauses joined not by order but by emotion. Let each phrase tumble into the next, driven by memory’s current rather than grammar’s restraint. Where clarity falters, resonance deepens — meaning is not lost but submerged.

Voice and consciousness should merge, often indistinguishable from one another. The world exists not as it is, but as it is remembered — distorted by guilt, longing, and the weight of what was left unsaid. Let contradictions stand. Let them breathe. Truth, in this weave, is not factual but felt.

###  Human Retrofit: Weave with Toni Morrison’s Lyrical Gravitas

Weave with reverence for memory and the voices it carries. Each line must hum with rhythm — the cadence of grief, joy, and survival interwoven. Let language move like a chorus: individual, intimate, yet collective. The weave is not told about people; it remembers them, as if the story itself were descended from them.

Favor lyrical phrasing and repetition that feels ritualistic, echoing the patterns of prayer or song. Allow prose to bend toward poetry when emotion demands it, but never lose its moral center. Every description should reveal more than surface — it should unveil ancestry, trauma, or redemption resting in the body’s gestures and the land’s silence.

Treat the supernatural and the ordinary as equal threads in the same fabric. Pain and beauty coexist — neither diminished, both necessary. The weave bears witness, not judgment.
